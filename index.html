▼ About Me

I am Yichuan Wang, a third-year undergraduate student from Institute for Interdisciplinary Information Sciences, Tsinghua University

My research interest lies broadly in Theoretical Computer Science (TCS)When I talk about TCS, I usually refer to Computational Complexity Theory.. I have previously published in space-bounded derandomization. However, what I appreciate the most is the paradigm of studying the connections between the power of a computational model and the hardness of its meta-computational problems, e.g. [Wil11] and [CIKK16]. I also look forward to seeing how developments in TCS will change human's understanding of our universe after 50 (or 100) years.

In Beijing, I was advised by Yilei Chen and Kuan Cheng. In Spring 2024, I am visiting UC Berkeley under the supervision of Lijie Chen and Avishay Tal.

I studied Mathematical Olympiad during high school and won the gold medal with the unique perfect score in the 62nd International Mathematical Olympiad (2021).
